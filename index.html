Interdisciplinary Research Infrastructure

We build reproducible neuroscience systems, from wet-lab experiments to AI-driven analysis

Neuroprocessing is an interdisciplinary neuroscience team developing open and reproducible research infrastructure across experimental biology, behavioral phenotyping, embedded neuroengineering, and computational neuroscience. We integrate animal models, molecular validation, and neural recording workflows with scalable AI pipelines to support rigorous, transparent, and translational neuroscience research.

Research Signals 2026 Program
  • Neuroengineering Systems: Arduino/Raspberry Pi-based acquisition, experimental automation, and scalable instrumentation.
  • Neural Data Analytics: EEG preprocessing, artifact modeling, SNR optimization, and reproducible signal pipelines.
  • Experimental Neuroscience: animal models, behavioral assays, histology, cell culture, and molecular workflows.
  • Multimodal AI & Validation: interpretable machine learning, benchmarking, documentation, and version-controlled reports.

Mission

Scientific rigor with a long-horizon neurotechnology vision.

Neuroprocessing exists to develop reproducible neuroscience workflows that connect experimental biology, behavioral studies, neural recording systems, and computational modeling. We build open tools, standardized protocols, and validated pipelines designed to support academic collaborations, grant-driven research, and long-term translation into scalable neurotechnology platforms.

Method Transparency

Every pipeline is designed with explicit preprocessing assumptions, quality-control standards, statistical validation, and reproducible reporting.

Open Collaboration

We collaborate with laboratories and interdisciplinary teams through shared protocols, open datasets, and co-developed experimental and computational tools.

Translational Roadmap

Our research outputs are structured to evolve into deployable neurotechnology systems with real-world scalability and future clinical relevance.
